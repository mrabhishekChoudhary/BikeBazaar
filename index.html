When choosing a bike size, consider your height and inseam measurement. Check the manufacturer's sizing charts and try out different models if possible. A bike that fits well ensures comfort and control while riding.

Regular maintenance includes checking tire pressure, lubricating the chain, inspecting brakes, and ensuring all bolts are tightened. A yearly tune-up by a professional can prolong the life of your bike and ensure safe operation.

Yes, you can ride in rainy conditions with proper precautions. Use fenders to prevent water splashes, wear bright clothing for visibility, and use lights. Reduce speed and increase braking distance to maintain control on wet surfaces.

Replace your bike helmet every 5-7 years, even if it hasn't been involved in a crash. Helmets deteriorate over time due to sweat, UV exposure, and general wear. Inspect it regularly for cracks, dents, or other signs of damage.
